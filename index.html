APPLIED DEEP LEARNING FOR 3D OBJECT DETECTION IN TRAFFIC SENSING IN REAL TIME

Detection, classification, and tracking of road users on public roads are crucial for automated driving systems, traffic analytics, and traffic control. However, variability of amount, dynamics and appearance presents huge challenges to perception systems. The challenges of illumination, clutter, or occlusion could also affect the performance of 3D object detection algorithms. Several sensor technologies (e.g. camera, radar, lidar) and numerous detection methods have been developed in a strive to achieve robust and efficient object detection in traffic. Stereovision-based sensors are attractive due to their low cost and high mechanical robustness combined with the possibility to perceive the surroundings in 3 dimensions. The downside is a need for complex algorithms on embedded hardware with limited computational power. Inspired by the maturity of 2D object detection algorithms based on Deep Neural Networks on RGB images (YOLO family) and the capability of extracting depth information in stereo vision sensors, in this thesis, we proposed an efficient algorithm for stereo vision sensors that is capable of detecting 3D objects of interest in the cities. We utilized YOLOv5 to extract 2D object patches from left and right images in stereo cameras. In the next step, these patch pairs are used to produce 3D point clouds by means of the stereo vision triangulation method. Within 3D space trimmed by the open and lightweight YOLO DNN above, the computational cost of stereo vision and following 3D detection algorithms are reduced. The final results obtained on the KITTI stereo vision dataset indicate that YOLO-First presented a promising lightweight computational cost algorithm for 3D object detection in real-time.
Keyworks: Deep Learning, 3D Object Detection, Stereo Vision, YOLO.
